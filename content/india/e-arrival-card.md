## Quick answer

The India e-Arrival Card is **free**. Foreign nationals, including OCI cardholders, submit it within the **72 hours before arrival**.

It is an arrival record, not permission to travel. A visa holder still needs the visa. An OCI cardholder still travels on OCI status. An Indian passport holder does not file the foreign-national arrival card.

## What the e-Arrival Card records

The form collects identity, passport, travel purpose, arrival, recent travel, India address, contact, emergency-contact, and declaration information. Use the passport you will present at Indian immigration.

The official service does not charge a filing fee. A site charging £15 or $59.99 is selling assistance with a government card that costs nothing.

## The 72-hour submission window

Submit during the 72 hours immediately before reaching India. For example, if the flight lands at 18:00 on July 20, the filing window starts at 18:00 on July 17.

Use the local arrival date shown by the airline, not the date the journey begins. Save the digital confirmation or reference after submission.

## e-Visa is a separate paid procedure

An e-Visa answers whether India permits you to make the trip. The e-Arrival Card records the arrival. Many travellers need both, while an OCI cardholder normally uses OCI status plus the free arrival card.

Official tourist e-Visa fees vary by nationality and validity. Examples verified in the July 9, 2026 official table include:

- **30 days:** USD $10 from April to June or $25 from July to March for Australia, Canada, the United States, the United Kingdom, and Japan.
- **1 year:** commonly USD $40. Japan is listed at $25.
- **5 years:** commonly USD $200. The United States is $160, the United Kingdom is $484, and Japan is $25.
- **Bank charge:** add 3% to the government fee.

Some nationalities are listed at USD $0. Always use the price shown for your nationality and selected validity on the official site at payment time.

## e-Visa entry ports are restricted

An e-Visa is valid for first entry only through the ports on the current official list. At verification, the list contained 33 named airports and 19 named seaports. Departure may be through any authorized Immigration Check Post.

Check the live port list before booking. A valid e-Visa does not make an unlisted first-entry port eligible.

## Check every character in the domain

The official visa system is `indianvisaonline.gov.in`. Read the ending from right to left: it must end in **`.gov.in`**. A domain containing words such as india, visa, government, online, or arrival is not official unless that exact suffix is present.

For example, `earrivalcardindia.com` and `indiaearrivalcardgo.com` look relevant but do not end in `.gov.in`. On July 14, 2026, they advertised £15 and $59.99 services for the free card.

Commercial e-Visa packages can also cost much more. One documented site advertised $99 for 30 days, $149 for 1 year, and $399 for 5 years. Those were bundled totals, not the standard government price table.
