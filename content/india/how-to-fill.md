The e-Arrival Card is free. Foreign travellers, including OCI cardholders, submit it within 72 hours before arrival. Use the passport and visa, e-Visa, or OCI record that apply to this trip.

The checker reads the 15 rules in `data/rules/india.json`. It runs in your browser and sends nothing to us. Broad local length checks are not published Indian government limits. The live form decides what can be submitted.

## Section 1: Identity

1. **Full name as in passport:** copy the complete Latin-script name from the passport used for entry. Keep every middle given name.
2. **Nationality or region:** select the nationality or region printed on that passport, not a transit country or place of residence.
3. **Passport number:** copy only the number from the photo page. Remove spaces and machine-readable-zone `<` filler marks.
4. **Visa, e-Visa, or OCI number:** use the travel permission for this trip. The crawlable official web page did not display this field on July 14, 2026, but Indian government mission guidance tells travellers to have visa or OCI details ready. The field may appear by route, channel, or prefilled record.

## Section 2: Visit

5. **Purpose of visit:** choose the activity that matches both the trip and your travel permission, such as tourism, business, medical treatment, conference, study, or employment.
6. **Date of arrival in India:** use the local date the flight reaches India. The official form displays `DD/MM/YYYY`; this checker uses `YYYY-MM-DD`. Submit within the preceding 72 hours.
7. **Flight number:** enter the airline code and flight number landing in India, such as `AI302`, not the booking reference. The crawlable web page did not display this field at verification, but government mission materials list flight details among the information to have ready. Display can vary by route, channel, or record.
8. **Countries visited in the last 6 days:** report every country requested for the previous 6 days. Choose `No Country Visited` only when no country applies.

## Section 3: India address

9. **Address in India:** enter the first full hotel, host, or residence address. A city name alone is not a complete address.
10. **State or union territory:** select the jurisdiction for that address. Ask the hotel or host if the booking does not make it clear.
11. **District:** select the district containing the address. District and city names are not always identical.

## Section 4: Contact

12. **Email:** use an inbox you control. Check spelling before submission so the confirmation does not go to the wrong address.
13. **Contact number:** include the country or region code and a number where you can be reached.
14. **Emergency contact number:** if supplied, use a reachable contact other than your own number and include the country or region code.

## Section 5: Declaration

15. **Truth and accuracy declaration:** review every answer before confirming. The official warning says false information may lead to denial of entry or legal consequences.

## After submission

Save the digital confirmation or reference. The record is checked with the travel document at immigration. It does not replace a visa, e-Visa, or OCI status.
