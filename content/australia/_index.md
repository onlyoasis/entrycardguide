There is **no official web application form for Australia ETA 601**. The only official ETA 601 application channel is the AustralianETA App from the Department of Home Affairs.

## Start here

### 1. Is Australia ETA free?

The ETA has no Visa Application Charge, but submitting it in the official App costs **AUD $20 per application**. Eligible European passport holders can often use the free eVisitor 651 website instead.

→ [Read the ETA 601 and eVisitor 651 overview](/australia/eta/)

### 2. How the AustralianETA App works

Download the official App, read the passport chip with NFC, take a live face photo, answer declaration questions, pay AUD $20, and wait for the result.

→ [Follow the six App steps](/australia/how-to-fill/)

### 3. Is iVisa the official Australia ETA site?

No. Compare the official routes with observed commercial charges from USD $89.99 to $200 for help with the free eVisitor 651.

## ETA 601 or eVisitor 651?

- **ETA 601:** for an ETA-eligible passport. Apply only in AustralianETA. There is no Visa Application Charge, but the App service charge is AUD $20.
- **eVisitor 651:** for an eligible European passport. Apply online through Home Affairs and ImmiAccount. The government application is free.
- **Both:** normally allow multiple visits during a 12-month period, with stays of up to 3 months per entry. An ETA ends sooner if its linked passport expires first.
