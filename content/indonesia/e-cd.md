## What the e-CD actually is

The All Indonesia arrival declaration is the current digital entry point for arriving travelers. It includes the customs declaration record formerly filed as the standalone e-CD: cash above limits, restricted goods, commercial merchandise, and excise goods.

It is free.

No one can charge you for it. The current official destination is `allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id`. The old standalone customs portal at `ecd.beacukai.go.id` still resolves, but travelers should start from All Indonesia.

## What about a visa?

The All Indonesia / e-CD declaration is **not** a visa. It is an arrival and customs declaration that is required regardless of your visa status. If you need a visa, you file the **e-VOA** (Electronic Visa on Arrival) separately at `evisa.imigrasi.go.id` for about $35 USD government fee. Different form, different fee rule.

## The problem

Search Google for *"Bali customs form"* or *"Indonesia arrival form"* and you'll see paid ads from middleman sites charging $19 to $79 USD for the free e-CD. Some of them submit the real form on your behalf. Some submit a different form (sometimes just an e-VOA when you didn't ask for one), or nothing at all.

The Indonesian government now routes travelers through **All Indonesia**. The declaration remains free; any site charging for the declaration is a middleman.

## Before you touch any form

Check the address bar. The current official entry point is <mark>allindonesia.imigrasi.go.id</mark>.

Sites using `.com`, `.org`, or `.net` with customs-style branding are commercial middlemen. Anyone can register those open suffixes. Only Indonesian government entities can register `.go.id`.

## Every field the official form asks

The e-CD has roughly 9 fields across 3 sections: traveler identity, trip details, and a customs declaration.

No photo upload required.

No payment required.

If a site asks for either, you are not on the official site.

## How to tell any e-CD site is not the real one

1. **Does the domain end in `.go.id`?** If no, it is not an Indonesian government site. Period.
2. **Does it ask for payment?** The e-CD is free. Any fee means a middleman.
3. **Does it ask you to upload a passport photo?** The official e-CD does not.
4. **Does the URL contain words like "bali", "indonesia", "customs", "official" but not `.go.id`?** Bait.
5. **Does the page have testimonials, trust badges, or countdown timers?** Government forms have none of these.
