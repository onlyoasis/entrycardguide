## Quick answer

UK ETA is **not free**. The Home Office charges **£20 for each applicant**.

- The fee started at **£10**.
- It rose to **£16 on April 9, 2025**.
- It rose again to **£20 on April 8, 2026**.

Every traveler needs a separate application, including a baby or child. A family of four pays £80. There is no family application or family discount.

## What UK ETA is

ETA stands for Electronic Travel Authorisation. It lets eligible visa-free passport holders request permission to travel to the United Kingdom.

ETA is **not a visa**. If your nationality or trip requires a visa, an ETA cannot replace it. Approval also does not guarantee entry. Border control makes the final admission decision.

## Who does not need an ETA

You do not need an ETA if you are a **British citizen or Irish citizen**. You also do not need one if you already have a UK visa or permission to live, work, or study in the UK.

Other travelers should check eligibility against the nationality shown on the passport they will use.

## Official website or official app

There are two official application routes:

1. Apply in a browser through `gov.uk/eta`.
2. Use the **UK ETA** app. Its developer or seller must be **Home Office**.

The app photographs the passport and face and may scan the passport chip. The GOV.UK online form is the official alternative if you cannot use the app.

## How long a decision takes

Most applicants receive a decision by email **within a day**. Allow **up to 3 working days** before travel. Another website or app cannot provide a faster Home Office decision.

Use an email address you control. Keep the decision email and its 16-digit ETA reference number.

## Validity and stay length

An ETA lasts **2 years or until the linked passport expires**, whichever comes first. A new passport requires a new ETA.

The authorization permits multiple journeys during its validity. Each visit is normally limited to **up to 6 months** and must follow the permitted visitor activities.

## Why search results show higher prices

Commercial application sites sell form-filling or review services. Prices observed on July 14, 2026 included **USD $100.99 at iVisa**, a stated **EUR 69 service fee at eta-united-kingdom.com**, and totals of **EUR 69, EUR 119, or EUR 169 at application-eta.uk**.

The Home Office fee remains £20. Paying a service does not create a family discount, improve the approval decision, or shorten the Home Office processing time.
