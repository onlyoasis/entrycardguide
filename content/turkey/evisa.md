## You may not need an e-Visa at all

Ordinary passport holders from the **United States, United Kingdom, Canada, and Australia** are currently visa-exempt for tourist visits of up to **90 days in any 180-day period**. Ordinary People's Republic of China passport holders have the same exemption from **January 2, 2026**.

Many other passports are exempt, including examples from the EU, Japan, South Korea, Hong Kong SAR, Singapore, and Malaysia. That sentence is not a complete list. Passport type and allowed stay can differ, so find the exact entry on the Ministry of Foreign Affairs page.

{{< official-link site="turkey.mfa_visa_info" >}}

If the MFA page says your passport is visa-exempt for this trip, stop. You do not need an e-Visa or a separate arrival form. The correct cost is zero.

## If your passport still needs an e-Visa

Use the government application site only after the exemption check.

{{< official-link site="turkey.evisa" >}}

The official flow asks for nationality or region of the travel document, document type, and planned arrival date first. Those answers determine whether the route is visa-exempt, directly eligible for an e-Visa, conditionally eligible, or not eligible for this online visa.

An e-Visa is a travel authorization, not a general entry card. Each traveller needs the result that matches the passport used at the border.

## How much is the Turkey e-Visa?

There is no single price that is accurate for every passport. The fee **varies by passport**, and the official system shows the live amount only after the eligibility choices are made.

Do not copy a price from a blog, an old screenshot, or another traveller's receipt. A visa-exempt passport has no e-Visa fee because no e-Visa is required. An eligible passport can receive a different official quote based on the current rules.

## Conditional e-Visa eligibility

Some nationalities, including **India, Bangladesh, Pakistan, and the Philippines**, may obtain a 30-day single-entry e-Visa only when the official result says they meet added conditions.

One common condition is holding a valid **Schengen, US, UK, or Ireland visa or residence permit**. That document alone does not guarantee eligibility. Read every condition on the live result, including any carrier, return-ticket, accommodation, or funds requirement displayed for the passport.

If you cannot meet a listed condition, do not pay a commercial site to bypass it. The middleman cannot change the Ministry's eligibility decision.

## What the application asks

The 18 documented fields cover:

- nationality, travel-document type, and arrival date;
- given names, surname, birth details, and parents' names;
- travel-document number, issue date, and expiry date;
- email, phone number, and residence address;
- supporting-document type, issuer, and expiry date when conditional eligibility applies.

## After payment

Use an email address you control. The official system sends the payment and download links there. Save the application reference, receipt, and issued PDF. Carry the e-Visa as a digital or printed copy with the same passport used in the application.

Before paying, check the address bar one more time. The government host is exactly `evisa.gov.tr`, ending in `.gov.tr`.
