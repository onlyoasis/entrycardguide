First check whether your route requires an eTA. It is normally for flying to Canada or connecting through a Canadian airport. Arrival by car, bus, train, or cruise ship does not require one. U.S. citizens and U.S. lawful permanent residents are also exempt; green card holders flying to Canada carry a valid passport plus a valid green card or other accepted proof of U.S. status.

If you do need an eTA, use the passport you will carry on the flight. The official fee is CAD $7, and an approved eTA can remain valid for up to 5 years or until that passport expires.

The checker below covers the 11 field groups in `data/rules/canada.json`. It runs in your browser and sends nothing to us. The live IRCC form controls the final questions and option lists.

Names accept the Roman-script spelling used by the official form, including French accents. Passport numbers use letters and numbers only in this local checker. IRCC says most passport numbers contain 8 to 11 characters, but this checker allows 1 to 20 because that is not a universal passport limit.

## Before payment

Confirm that the application is on `canada.ca` and the charge is CAD $7. iVisa advertised prices from USD $89.99, while canadavisas.net displayed a USD $99 final price on July 14, 2026. Those totals include commercial help, not a higher IRCC fee.

Use an email address you control. IRCC sends the decision and any document request there. After approval, compare the passport number in the email with the passport photo page. If they differ, IRCC requires a new application.

The eTA supports multiple trips but remains linked to the application passport. A replacement passport requires a new eTA.
